How this prediction was made

This model is not a black box. Below is the exact pipeline, and the heatmap above shows the brain regions that drove the decision (red = most influential).

  1. Load scan — read the uploaded volume ({{ explanation.original_shape }} voxels).
  2. Normalize intensity — robust scaling to [0, 1], clipped at the {{ explanation.clip_percentiles[0] }}–{{ explanation.clip_percentiles[1] }} percentiles so bright white-matter hyperintensities are preserved.
  3. Resample — trilinear resize to a fixed {{ explanation.processed_shape }} grid for the network.
  4. 3D CNN feature extraction — four Conv3D blocks detect 3D lesion patterns; global max-pooling keeps small focal signals.
  5. Fuse with clinical data — the questionnaire answers are encoded and concatenated with the MRI features, so history and symptoms inform the verdict alongside the scan.
  6. Classify & explain — a dense head outputs the probabilities above; Grad-CAM back-projects the decision onto the most influential slice (#{{ explanation.slice_index }}, ~{{ explanation.attention_pct }}% of it strongly attended).
